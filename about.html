About Fran

The heart behind every bouquet

Francine Miller

Francine “Fran” Miller grew up surrounded by her grandmother's garden in Sonoma County, where she first fell in love with the language of flowers. After earning a degree in Horticulture from UC Davis and apprenticing at three Bay Area studios, she opened Fran's Flowers in 1995 at the age of 28 with nothing more than a rented storefront, a cooler full of dahlias, and a deep belief that flowers can change a person's day.

Three decades later, that belief has never wavered. Fran still arrives at the San Francisco Flower Mart before dawn every Tuesday and Friday to hand-select seasonal stems — sniffing, squeezing, and scrutinizing each bunch before it earns a place in her shop.

Beyond the artistry, Fran is a committed community member. Since 2001 the shop has donated 10% of every sale to organizations fighting homelessness across the Bay Area, contributing over $200,000 to date. She also mentors aspiring florists through a paid apprenticeship program she launched in 2018.

When she's not arranging flowers or advocating for her neighborhood, you'll find Fran hiking the Marin Headlands with her rescue greyhound, Tulip.

Thirty Years of Blooms

  1. 1995

    Grand Opening

    Fran opens the doors of her first Valencia Street storefront with a hand-painted sign and three dozen mismatched vases.

  2. 2001

    Community First

    Launches the 10% giving pledge, donating to Bay Area homeless services — a commitment that has never lapsed.

  3. 2010

    Expansion

    Moves into the larger corner space at 123 Valencia, doubling the studio and adding a dedicated workshop for events.

  4. 2018

    Apprenticeship Program

    Fran begins paying it forward, launching a structured mentorship for emerging florists from under-resourced backgrounds.

  5. 2025

    Thirty & Thriving

    Celebrates three decades in business with the same passion, the same address, and a whole lot more experience.
